OneCarbon is developing a probiotic supplement that supports the cellular pathway consistently impaired in Alzheimer's brains — built on peer-reviewed science from the University of Cambridge.

The problem

One in three people aged 85+ lives with Alzheimer's. There is still no scalable prevention strategy.

Disease-modifying drugs like lecanemab and donanemab slow cognitive decline in a narrow early-stage population, requiring IV infusions and MRI monitoring. Vitamins and supplements lack the targeting or bioavailability to reach the affected pathways. A fundamental gap remains: a safe, accessible intervention that addresses the upstream biology of the disease.

The science

One-carbon metabolism — a pathway consistently impaired in Alzheimer's

Dr Yizhou Yu's research at the University of Cambridge showed that certain neurons survive Alzheimer's while others do not. The answer points to the one-carbon metabolic pathway — a cellular process involved in energy production, DNA repair, and neurotransmitter synthesis. This pathway is consistently disrupted in Alzheimer's brains, and restoring its activity is neuroprotective in animal models.

Five peer-reviewed papers underpin our approach, all from Cambridge. They establish both the mechanistic case and the translational rationale for supporting this pathway.

The product

1C-01 — a probiotic approach to one-carbon support

1C-01 is a food-grade probiotic supplement designed to support one-carbon metabolism. The details of the mechanism are subject to ongoing IP development. It is not a drug and makes no disease claims.

Clinical validation

PROFILE — Phase I pre-post trial, currently enrolling

The PROFILE trial is a pre-post study assessing safety and biomarker effects of 1C-01 in healthy adults aged 50–65. Participants complete a cognitive battery and provide biomarker samples at baseline and after a 60-day course. We are raising £800K to complete the trial and support the next stage of development.

Published research

The science behind 1C-01

Five peer-reviewed papers from Cambridge underpin what we are building. Each describes one aspect of the pathway we are targeting. The details of how 1C-01 works remain unpublished due to patents in preparation.

2024
Yu et al. — Enhancing mitochondrial one-carbon metabolism is neuroprotective in Alzheimer's disease models
Boosting the one-carbon pathway improved mitochondrial function in AD models — the core mechanistic rationale behind 1C-01.
nature.com/articles/s41419-024-07179-3
2024
Yu & Martins — Mitochondrial one-carbon metabolism and Alzheimer's disease
Five key metabolic processes disrupted in Alzheimer's — including redox balance and NAD⁺ availability — all converge on the one-carbon pathway. SAM, the brain's primary methyl donor, is severely depleted in AD brains.
2025
Yu et al. — Increased nucleotide metabolism alleviates Alzheimer's disease pathology
Phosphorylated nucleotides are depleted in AD models; restoring them reduced oxidative DNA damage and improved mitochondrial function.
nature.com/articles/s41419-025-08066-1
2021
Yu et al. — Parp mutations protect from mitochondrial toxicity in Alzheimer's disease
Metabolomic analysis of AD models identified depleted NAD⁺ as a key driver of neuronal energy failure — restoring NAD⁺ availability is neuroprotective.
nature.com/articles/s41419-021-03926-y
2025
Yu et al. — Amyloid-β moderates sleep duration through NAD⁺-linked redox metabolism
Sleep disruption in Alzheimer's is mechanistically linked to NAD⁺-dependent metabolism — the same pathway 1C-01 targets. bioRxiv 2025.
biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/2025.02.26.640405v1
