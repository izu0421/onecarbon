Why We Built 1C-01

March 2026 · Product

In this article

  1. The finding
  2. Why a probiotic?
  3. Where we are now

The question that started everything was deceptively simple: how do neurons protect themselves from Alzheimer's disease? Our co-founder Dr Yizhou Yu spent years at the University of Cambridge trying to answer it — and what he found pointed to a part of cellular biology that had been largely overlooked in the context of neurodegeneration.

The finding

In brief: Research from Cambridge showed that neurons naturally upregulate the one-carbon metabolic cycle under stress — and that this same cycle is severely impaired in Alzheimer's brains, pointing to a promising target for neuroprotection.

Neurons under stress upregulate the one-carbon metabolic cycle. This cycle, responsible for producing key methyl donors and supporting mitochondrial function, appears to be a natural protective response. In Alzheimer's brains, this same cycle is severely impaired.

The implication was clear: if you could support one-carbon metabolism consistently and safely, you might be able to slow the biological processes that lead to neurodegeneration. The challenge was how to do it in a way that was practical, affordable, and could be taken every day.

Why a probiotic?

In brief: Direct supplementation of one-carbon metabolites has absorption limitations, so OneCarbon instead took a probiotic approach — developing 1C-01 to support the one-carbon pathway in the gut in a sustained, accessible way.

Supplementing directly with one-carbon metabolites is possible, but absorption is limited and dosing is imprecise. We took a probiotic approach: the gut is a natural site of metabolite production, and molecules produced there can enter systemic circulation.

We developed 1C-01 as a probiotic designed to support one-carbon metabolism in the gut continuously and sustainably. The details of how 1C-01 works are subject to ongoing scientific and intellectual property development.

Where we are now

In brief: 1C-01 is currently being evaluated for safety and biomarker effects in the PROFILE Phase I trial in healthy adults — the published science behind the approach is available on the Research page.

1C-01 is not yet proven to prevent or treat any disease. We are currently investigating its safety and biomarker effects in healthy adults through our PROFILE Phase I trial. The published science underpinning our approach is available on the Research page.
