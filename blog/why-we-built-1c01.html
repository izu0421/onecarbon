Do you actually need another supplement?

Food is the foundation. Can we do more for our pathways? A reasonable thing to ask of any supplement is whether you actually need it at all.

Here's how we think about it — including where the science is settled, and where it isn't.

Diet vs. supplementation

Why not simply eat well?

Food is the foundation of health, and nothing we make is intended to replace a nutrient-dense diet. But relying on diet alone is a blunt way to target a highly specific biochemical pathway — for two reasons.

The age decline

Folate, one of the vital raw materials for your one-carbon pathway, tends to decline as we age — even on a sensible diet. Lower folate has been linked to faster brain ageing, so keeping levels steady matters.

The raw-material bottleneck

Eating more folate doesn't guarantee your body can use it where it's needed most. Cooking destroys a good portion of it, and your body still has to process and convert what remains.

The better question

Why not just take a folate pill?

This is the better question — and it's the problem that shaped our company. Walk into any shop and you'll find pills containing folic acid, the synthetic form of folate. Standard pills present a few challenges.

Spike-and-crash delivery

A pill delivers a single dose that peaks quickly in the bloodstream, then clears out. It's a one-off drop, rather than the slow, steady supply your cells rely on.

The conversion difference

A meaningful share of people carry a variation in the MTHFR gene that means their bodies convert synthetic folic acid less efficiently into the active form the brain uses. For them, a standard pill may be a less efficient route than a preformed, active metabolite.

The downstream problem

Folate is only the starting input. Your one-carbon pathway relies on a cascade of downstream metabolites to keep energy, cellular repair and other processes running.

Your gut, meanwhile, is already one of the body's primary natural producers of these active molecules. So rather than flooding your system from outside with a synthetic, hard-to-convert ingredient — we asked a simple question. Can we support the production line your body already runs?
